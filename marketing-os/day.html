Marketing Operating System · A Day in the Life · v4

One foreman.
One office manager.
Five published artifacts.

The day-to-day reality of operating the Marketing Operating System, told through one Tuesday afternoon pour at Spring Creek Commerce Pad. Foreman never logs in. Office manager spends 15 minutes with her morning coffee. Owner is invisible to the system except when judgment is needed.

Persona 01
Foreman
Tony, Manny, the crew leads
Surface: the phone they already have. One contact saved as "Jones Marketing." Texts a number when something good happens. That's it — no app, no login, no portal.
Daily commitment: ~15 sec / event
Persona 02
Office manager
Linda, or whoever runs the front office
Surface: a bookmarked web app. Inbox-style queue of pending drafts (field intakes, review replies, lead auto-replies, SEO pages). Edit / approve / reject. That's the whole interface.
Daily commitment: ~15 min / day
Persona 03
Owner
Steve, Aaron — leadership
Surface: push notifications + Friday digest email. Pulled in only on 1-2★ reviews, hot leads, and once-weekly performance. Almost never logs into the system itself.
Daily commitment: ~30 sec / week

Tony's total time invested: 18 seconds.

No app to download. No password to remember. The Jones Marketing number is saved in his contacts — same as the office line, the dispatch line, the equipment yard.

If he doesn't text, nothing happens. No nag emails, no "you missed your daily post" reminders. The system only fires when the field reports something worth reporting.

04:47:03 PM System · Twilio webhook

OS receives the text + photos

Twilio fires the inbound webhook to the Marketing Operating System. Photos are pulled into media storage. The Field-to-Content agent is dispatched with Tony's text + photo references + tenant config (Jones brand voice).
